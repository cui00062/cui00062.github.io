About Me

I have been thinking that photography is the way of life. Photographers arrange the elements in an artistic way in a picture and call it composition. They shoot it and make it look like and feel like the way it should be in his/her mind and present to all. In fact, both life and photography are colorful, excited, elegant. Of course, sometimes both are boring, bitter and even brutal. That is how life goes. It might be beyond many people’s understanding that a teacher and researcher with Ph.D degree in Chemistry from Queen’s University and strong academic background like me will be addicted to photography and further shift to interactive media design at Algonquin College. To me, however, both are very interesting and no contradiction. Both needs imagination, both needs commitment, and both brings us tears and joyful. I want to capture the beauty all around us, I want to tell good stories using my camera, I also want to turn concepts into visual arts and incorporate great images to digital graphics full of creative imagination with professional eyes and insight. If photography and graphic design keep inspiring me, and I do have the talents, then why not experience more and go deeper before too late? Curious about my story and want me to join you? Yi Cui, wins Bronze in the 2019 TIPF International Photography Contest!

A huge congratulations to Yi Cui for his image “Heavenly Horse Running in Kalajun Prairie in Xinjiang”, which won the Bronze medal in the theme “China”!!

The 2019 contest received 4176 images from 22 organizations around the world. The jury was composed of eight experienced senior judges from CAPA. Rod Trider, who has served as the leader and consultant of the judging team three consecutive times, highly praised the overall level of submissions. After much deliberation, the jury elected 78 awards. Amongst these very talented participants, Yi’s beautiful image of the running horses received a Bronze medal – well done, Yi, we are very proud of your accomplishment!
